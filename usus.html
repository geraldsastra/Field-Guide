Sate


USUS


Origin: Java Island



Sate Usus is a type of satay made from chicken intestines. This type of satay is commonly eaten with a type of Indonesian soup or rice porridge. This satay is also not grilled, but saut√©ed in spices and then skewered using bamboo skewers. Sate Usus also comes boiled, and then it is fried before serving. It is most commonly sold by street vendors due to its cheap price. Variations of Sate Usus also exists with different types of animal intestines like pork or beef. Since it is a side dish, it does not come with a specific sauce unlike other types of satays.
